Corporate sustainability in 2026 is no longer voluntary. The SEC climate disclosure rules, Europe's CSRD, and supply chain decarbonization pressure from large enterprise buyers have pushed carbon accounting from a nice-to-have into a compliance requirement for thousands of companies that had never measured their emissions before.

The challenge is that measuring a company's carbon footprint - especially Scope 3 value chain emissions - is genuinely hard. It requires pulling data from financial systems, energy providers, logistics partners, and upstream suppliers, then applying the right emission factor methodologies, then producing outputs that meet the specific format requirements of CDP, TCFD, GRI, and CSRD. This is a data and methodology problem, which is exactly the type of problem AI platforms are now solving. The best tools turn a 12-month measurement project into a structured workflow that produces a defensible, auditable inventory in weeks.

- **Best for developers:** Climatiq - emission factor API for embedding carbon calculation into any product or data pipeline

## The 5 Best AI Climate and Sustainability Tools in 2026

### 1. Watershed - The Enterprise Carbon Platform

Watershed is the most complete carbon management platform for mid-market and enterprise companies. The platform connects directly to financial systems, energy bills, supplier data, and logistics providers to pull the raw activity data that underpins emissions calculations, then applies GHG Protocol methodology to produce an emissions inventory that meets CDP, TCFD, and SEC climate disclosure requirements.

Where Watershed differentiates most clearly is Scope 3 measurement. Scope 3 - the upstream and downstream emissions in a company's value chain - typically represents 70 to 90 percent of a company's total footprint but is the hardest category to measure credibly. Watershed's supplier data collection tools, spend-based estimation models, and primary data prioritization workflows give sustainability teams a defensible Scope 3 inventory rather than rough estimates that will not survive external audit.

The reduction program module extends beyond measurement into action: SBTi-aligned target setting, initiative tracking for energy efficiency and renewable procurement projects, and a carbon market integration for purchasing verified carbon credits. For companies that need to show a credible path from measurement to net-zero target to progress reporting, Watershed handles the full chain in one platform.

**Pricing:** Enterprise pricing based on company revenue and reporting complexity. Custom engagement required. Not designed for companies under 200 employees.

**Best for:** Mid-market and enterprise companies across technology, retail, manufacturing, and financial services that need audit-ready Scope 3 and a structured net-zero program.

**Limitation:** Enterprise-only pricing and a structured onboarding engagement make Watershed inaccessible for small businesses or solo sustainability practitioners.

### 2. Persefoni - Financial-Grade Carbon Accounting

Persefoni is built around a specific insight: as carbon data faces the same scrutiny as financial data, it needs to be managed with the same rigor. The platform applies Generally Accepted Emissions Accounting Principles - GAEAP - to produce emissions inventories that are structured like financial accounts: auditable, comparable across periods, and defensible to external assurance providers.

The standout feature is the PCAF financed emissions module. Banks, asset managers, and insurance companies are required under the Partnership for Carbon Accounting Financials standard to measure the emissions associated with their loan and investment portfolios. Persefoni built this module specifically for financial institutions navigating that requirement - a use case that most carbon platforms do not address at all.

For listed companies facing mandatory CSRD reporting in Europe or SEC climate rules in the US, Persefoni produces structured reporting outputs aligned to both frameworks. The assurance module generates the documentation package external auditors need to verify the emissions inventory - reflecting where the market is heading as carbon assurance becomes standard practice.

**Pricing:** Enterprise pricing requires a sales engagement. Designed for large corporates and financial institutions.

**Best for:** Publicly listed companies, large private enterprises, banks, asset managers, and insurance companies where carbon data will face external assurance.

**Limitation:** Platform depth requires a meaningful implementation investment. The financial institution module requires familiarity with PCAF methodology to use correctly.

### 3. Greenly - Carbon Accounting Made Accessible for SMBs

Greenly occupies a specific and important position: enterprise-level accuracy in a product designed for companies that cannot afford enterprise-level pricing or complexity. The platform connects to accounting software - QuickBooks, Xero, Sage - and bank transactions to extract spend data, applies emission factors, and produces a Scope 1, 2, and 3 carbon inventory that a non-specialist manager can interpret from the dashboard.

The onboarding experience is designed to produce a first emissions estimate within a working day from connected financial data. That speed matters for SMBs responding to a specific trigger: an enterprise customer has added a carbon footprint question to their procurement qualification process, and the supplier needs a credible answer within weeks rather than months.

An employee engagement module allows companies to communicate their climate program internally and track individual reduction actions. A marketplace connects customers with climate experts and offset providers. For SMBs that need to respond to customer or investor carbon disclosure requests without investing six figures in an enterprise platform, Greenly is consistently the strongest option in the market.

**Pricing:** Starting around $500 per year for small companies, scaling with company size and reporting complexity.

**Best for:** SMBs responding to enterprise customer procurement carbon requirements, companies measuring their first carbon footprint, and businesses building an internal sustainability culture.

**Limitation:** Spend-based calculation is less accurate than primary data collection for Scope 3 materiality. Not a full ESG platform for companies needing governance and social metrics alongside carbon.

### 4. Plan A - Full ESG Coverage for European Companies

Plan A takes a broader view than carbon-only platforms. The tool covers Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions measurement, ESG data management across environmental, social, and governance metrics, and reporting frameworks including GRI, SASB, TCFD, CDP, and CSRD. For sustainability managers at companies that need to report on worker welfare, board governance, and biodiversity alongside carbon emissions, Plan A's breadth is a real differentiator.

The decarbonization module allows sustainability teams to build reduction roadmaps with initiative tracking, science-based target alignment, and scenario modeling that projects how different combinations of initiatives affect the emissions trajectory toward 2030 and 2050 targets. For sustainability teams presenting net-zero plans to boards and investors, the scenario modeling feature is particularly useful for building the narrative from current baseline to target year.

Plan A is headquartered in Berlin and is particularly well-positioned for European companies navigating CSRD requirements. The platform integrates with SAP, Microsoft Dynamics, and other ERP systems for automated data collection. A marketplace of climate consultants and assurance providers is accessible within the platform for companies that need external support alongside the software.

**Pricing:** Modular and scales by user count, reporting frameworks required, and data integration complexity. Requires a sales engagement.

**Best for:** European companies navigating CSRD, sustainability managers reporting across multiple ESG frameworks, and companies that need decarbonization scenario modeling alongside measurement.

**Limitation:** Less depth for companies primarily subject to US regulatory requirements. The breadth of ESG coverage means less depth in any single area compared to specialist carbon platforms.

### 5. Climatiq - The Carbon Calculation API for Developers

Climatiq is different from every other platform on this list. It is not a sustainability platform for companies measuring their own footprint - it is an emission factor API that enables developers and product teams to add accurate carbon calculation to any application, workflow, or data pipeline.

The API provides access to a curated, regularly-updated database of emission factors from IPCC, IEA, EPA, DEFRA, and other authoritative sources, covering electricity generation by grid and hour, freight and logistics by mode and distance, air travel by route and class, materials and manufacturing processes, and hundreds of other activity categories. Developers embed the API to calculate the carbon footprint of each shipment in a logistics platform, show the emissions of each business trip in an expense management tool, or display the carbon intensity of purchases in a fintech app.

Climatiq handles the emission factor management and methodology updates so product teams focus on building the feature rather than maintaining the data. The database covers over 50,000 emission factors across 150+ countries. An Estimates endpoint handles common calculation patterns without requiring the developer to know the underlying methodology details.

**Pricing:** Free tier covers 100 API calls per month for prototyping. Paid plans start at $99/month for higher volumes and commercial usage.

**Best for:** Developers embedding carbon calculation into logistics, travel, e-commerce, or procurement platforms; product teams building sustainability features into ERP or finance software.

**Limitation:** Developer-focused product - non-technical sustainability teams need either the no-code interface or a developer to get value. Emission factor accuracy has inherent uncertainty in some categories.

## The Bottom Line

The most important first step for any company is choosing the right scope of ambition. Companies that need to measure and disclose carbon to meet regulatory or customer requirements should start with a focused carbon accounting platform - Greenly for SMBs, Watershed or Persefoni for enterprises. Companies that need to report across all ESG pillars to CSRD should start with Plan A or IBM Envizi.

The common mistake is choosing a platform for the features it advertises rather than the compliance framework it actually needs to satisfy. A CSRD report requires specific data points about governance, human rights, and biodiversity that a carbon-only platform will not collect. An SEC climate filing requires emissions data structured to specific definitions that a lightweight SMB tool may not produce. Match the platform to the compliance framework first.

The next two years will see dramatic market consolidation as carbon assurance requirements raise the bar for what counts as a credible inventory. Platforms that can produce audit-ready data with external assurance documentation - primarily Watershed and Persefoni at the enterprise end, and Plan A for European companies - are best positioned for the regulatory environment that is already in place. Companies that invest in measurement infrastructure now will be significantly ahead of competitors who are still estimating from spend data when auditors start asking questions.
