Data analysis used to require a data scientist. In 2026, it requires a subscription.

The new generation of AI data analysis tools puts predictive models, natural language queries, and automated insights in the hands of analysts, marketers, and operations teams - no Python required. Whether you need to query a database in plain English, build a no-code ML model, or analyze millions of rows in a browser tab, there is now a purpose-built tool for it.

This guide covers the 10 best AI data analysis tools across five categories: AI spreadsheets, no-code machine learning, natural language SQL, collaborative notebooks, and big data exploration.

---

## AI Spreadsheets

### Rows

Rows is a modern spreadsheet with AI built directly into cells. You can call GPT functions inside formulas to classify customer feedback, summarize text columns, or generate content at scale across thousands of rows. Native connectors to Stripe, HubSpot, Google Analytics, and 50+ other data sources mean data flows in automatically - no manual export-import cycles.

Rows also publishes analyses as shareable interactive reports, making it a lightweight alternative to a full BI tool for teams that think in spreadsheets.

**Best for:** Data teams that live in spreadsheets but need richer analysis and live data connections.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Paid plans from $59/month.

### Formula Bot

Formula Bot translates plain English into Excel and Google Sheets formulas. Describe what you want - "sum sales where region is North and month is Q1" - and it generates the formula, including complex nested IF statements, VLOOKUP, XLOOKUP, and array formulas.

Beyond formula generation, Formula Bot also generates VBA macros, SQL queries, and Python code from natural language, making it useful for analysts who work across multiple tools.

**Best for:** Analysts who spend time debugging formulas or need to work faster in Excel without learning advanced syntax.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Pro from $9/month.

## No-Code Machine Learning

### Akkio

Akkio is a no-code machine learning platform designed for business analysts and marketing teams. Upload a CSV, select a target column (churn, revenue, conversion), and Akkio trains a predictive model in minutes. It handles feature selection, model training, and deployment automatically.

The platform integrates with HubSpot, Salesforce, and Google Sheets so predictions flow directly into CRM records. A chat interface lets you ask questions about your data in plain English, with Akkio responding with charts and summaries.

**Best for:** Marketing and sales teams that want predictive scoring without hiring a data scientist.

**Pricing:** Paid plans from $49/month. Free trial available.

### Obviously AI

Obviously AI focuses on instant predictive analytics from structured data. Upload any dataset and select a prediction target - the platform trains multiple models, selects the best performer, and explains the key drivers in plain language. Results are presented in an executive-friendly dashboard rather than a Jupyter notebook.

The one-click API deployment means predictions can be embedded in other applications without engineering support, making it genuinely useful for small teams.

**Best for:** Business teams that need fast predictive models with explainable results for stakeholder presentations.

**Pricing:** Plans from $75/month.

### Polymer

Polymer transforms spreadsheet data into interactive, shareable dashboards and apps without writing a line of code. Upload a Google Sheet or CSV and Polymer generates visualizations, enables filtering, and publishes a shareable URL. AI surfaces trends and anomalies automatically.

Unlike traditional BI tools, Polymer is built for speed and shareability - an analysis is ready to share within minutes of upload, without dashboard configuration or SQL knowledge.

**Best for:** Teams that need to share data insights with non-technical stakeholders quickly, without BI tool complexity.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Paid from $29/month.

## Natural Language SQL

### Outerbase

Outerbase is a modern database interface that lets teams query databases in plain English. Connect your PostgreSQL, MySQL, or BigQuery database and ask questions in natural language - Outerbase generates the SQL, runs it, and returns results as charts or tables.

The collaborative workspace lets teams share queries, build dashboards, and comment on data - replacing the ad hoc "can you pull this for me" requests that slow down data teams. Non-technical teammates can get data answers without waiting for a data analyst.

**Best for:** Product and operations teams that need database access without SQL knowledge, and data teams that want a collaborative query interface.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Paid from $25/month.

### Seek AI

Seek AI focuses specifically on natural language SQL for enterprise data warehouses. Connect Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift, or Databricks and ask questions in plain English. Seek AI generates SQL, explains the query in plain language, and returns results with automatic visualizations.

The platform includes query validation to catch incorrect joins and ambiguous column references before they return wrong answers - a critical feature for business users who may not recognize a SQL error in the output.

**Best for:** Business intelligence teams and data analysts at companies with large data warehouse environments.

**Pricing:** Custom enterprise pricing.

### Hal9

Hal9 provides a conversational AI interface for data analysis across SQL databases and spreadsheets. Ask questions, get visualizations, and drill down with follow-up questions - all without leaving the chat interface. Hal9 connects to most SQL databases and supports CSV uploads for one-off analyses.

The tool is particularly effective for data exploration sessions where the next question depends on the previous answer, mimicking the iterative workflow of an analyst conversation.

**Best for:** Analysts who want a conversational interface for exploratory data analysis without writing SQL.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Paid plans for team use.

## Collaborative Notebooks

### Deepnote

Deepnote is a collaborative data science notebook designed for teams. Built on Jupyter-compatible Python, SQL, and R, Deepnote adds real-time collaboration (Google Docs-style), scheduled execution, and publishing to shareable data apps - all features that standard Jupyter notebooks lack.

The AI assistant inside Deepnote generates code, explains outputs, and helps debug errors in context. For data science teams that want Jupyter's power with Notion's collaboration, Deepnote is the most mature option.

**Best for:** Data science and analytics teams that need collaborative notebooks with scheduling, AI assistance, and stakeholder-friendly output.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Teams plans from $39/month.

## Big Data Exploration

### Gigasheet

Gigasheet is a browser-based spreadsheet for massive files - handling CSVs, JSON, and log files up to billions of rows without any database setup or coding. For security operations and data engineering teams that routinely work with large log files and threat intelligence feeds, Gigasheet removes the technical barrier of big data analysis.

Filter, group, deduplicate, and pivot enormous datasets entirely in the browser. Shareable filtered views let teams collaborate on large files without email attachments or database access.

**Best for:** Security analysts, data engineers, and operations teams who work with large log, event, and security data files regularly.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Paid plans from $99/month.

## How to Choose

**You need predictions from your data:** Akkio or Obviously AI. Both handle model training without code. Akkio integrates with CRM for direct scoring; Obviously AI is faster for one-off predictive analyses.

**You live in spreadsheets:** Rows if you need live data connections and AI in cells. Formula Bot if your bottleneck is formula and SQL generation in Excel or Google Sheets.

**You need database access without SQL:** Outerbase for collaborative team access. Seek AI for enterprise data warehouses where query accuracy is critical. Hal9 for conversational exploratory analysis.

**You work with large files:** Gigasheet handles what Excel, Google Sheets, and most BI tools cannot - billion-row files in a browser tab.

**You need a collaborative notebook:** Deepnote is the best-in-class option for data science teams that want Jupyter with team collaboration and scheduling.

**You need shareable dashboards fast:** Polymer turns a CSV into a shareable interactive dashboard in minutes without BI tool configuration.

The right tool depends on your role. For business analysts who think in spreadsheets: Rows and Formula Bot. For data scientists who code: Deepnote. For everyone who wants answers from data without either: Akkio, Outerbase, and Hal9.
