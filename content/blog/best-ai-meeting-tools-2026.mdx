If you spend more than two hours a day in video calls, you already know the problem: you finish a meeting, open a blank doc, and try to reconstruct what was decided. By the time you type it up, the details are fuzzy and the action items are incomplete.

AI meeting tools solve this at the source. They join your calls, transcribe everything in real time, identify action items and decisions automatically, and deliver a structured summary the moment the call ends. The best ones go further - they coach sales reps, score engagement, sync to your CRM, and let you search months of conversations by keyword.

This guide covers the top AI meeting tools for 2026, from lightweight personal assistants to enterprise-grade conversation intelligence platforms.

---

## What Makes a Great AI Meeting Tool?

Before the list, here is what separates genuinely useful tools from basic recorders:

- **Transcript accuracy** - Can it handle accents, crosstalk, and technical jargon? Average tools fall apart; great tools stay readable.
- **Summary quality** - Does the summary reflect what actually mattered, or is it a verbose recap of everything said?
- **Action item detection** - Does it reliably catch commitments ("I'll send that by Friday") without flooding you with false positives?
- **Integration depth** - Does it push to Slack, CRM, Notion, or your project management tool automatically?
- **Privacy and permissions** - Does it join as a visible bot, or can it operate without one? This matters for client-facing calls.

## Top AI Meeting Tools for 2026

### 1. Grain - Best for Video Clip Sharing

Grain treats meetings like video content. After recording and transcribing your call, it identifies key moments and lets you clip them into short shareable videos - a quote from a customer, a piece of feedback, a key decision. You can share these clips directly in Slack, Notion, or via link without anyone needing to watch the full recording.

This is particularly powerful for sales and product teams. Customer success teams clip objections to share with product. Managers clip coaching moments for async review. User researchers build highlight reels from interview recordings.

Grain integrates with Salesforce and HubSpot to sync call summaries and key moments to contact records automatically. The free plan includes unlimited recordings with a 10-hour storage limit, making it one of the most generous entry points available.

**Best for:** Product, sales, and research teams who need to share specific meeting moments, not just summaries.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Paid plans from $15/user/month.

---

### 2. Read AI - Best for Meeting Engagement Analytics

Read AI goes beyond notes. It analyses meeting dynamics in real time - tracking participation patterns, speaking time distribution, sentiment shifts, and engagement levels across the whole call. You get an engagement score for each meeting along with the transcript, summary, and action items.

For managers running many one-on-ones or team leads who want to improve how their meetings run, this data is genuinely useful. Read AI also works without requiring a bot to join the call - it processes audio directly - which removes the awkwardness of a visible AI participant in client meetings.

The real-time summary updates throughout the call, not just after, so participants can verify shared understanding before disconnecting.

**Best for:** Managers and leadership teams who want data on meeting quality, not just meeting content.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Paid from $19.75/user/month.

---

### 3. Jamie - Best Lightweight Assistant (No Bot Required)

Jamie is a Mac and Windows desktop app that transcribes and summarises meetings without ever joining as a bot. It works by processing audio locally or through the microphone, which means it works with any conferencing tool - Zoom, Teams, Google Meet, WhatsApp calls, and even in-person conversations.

The summaries are concise and well-structured, with a clear focus on decisions and next steps rather than a full transcript dump. For professionals who want a clean, private meeting assistant that stays invisible to other participants, Jamie is one of the cleanest implementations available.

**Best for:** Consultants, executives, and anyone who wants an invisible assistant that works across all meeting formats.

**Pricing:** Free plan with limited summaries. Paid from $24/month.

---

### 4. Meetgeek - Best for Team Meeting Automation

Meetgeek is built for teams that want zero friction on meeting documentation. It joins calls automatically based on calendar invite patterns, transcribes and summarises, and then distributes the notes to the right channels without any manual steps. You configure it once and it handles every recurring meeting going forward.

The template library covers common meeting types - one-on-ones, sprint planning, sales calls, customer success check-ins - with pre-built agendas and summary formats for each. Integration with Slack, Notion, HubSpot, Trello, and Asana means notes appear where your team already works.

**Best for:** Operations teams and managers who want meeting notes fully automated without any per-meeting setup.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Paid from $15/user/month.

### 5. Sembly AI - Best for Multi-Language Teams

Sembly AI is one of the strongest options for international teams working across multiple languages. It transcribes and summarises meetings in over 40 languages, and can handle mixed-language conversations where participants switch between languages mid-call.

Beyond transcription, Sembly generates structured meeting reports with decisions, risks, and commitments flagged separately from general discussion. The AI identifies speaker roles based on the topics they raise, which improves summary quality on calls with multiple participants.

**Best for:** Global teams and companies with multilingual customers or operations.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Paid from $15/user/month.

### 6. Chorus (by ZoomInfo) - Best for Enterprise Sales Teams

Chorus is a conversation intelligence platform designed for revenue teams at scale. It records, transcribes, and analyses sales calls with AI trained specifically on sales language - identifying objections, competitor mentions, pricing discussions, and buying signals automatically.

Managers can review calls filtered by topic, track whether reps are following the sales methodology, and identify the specific patterns that appear in won versus lost deals. Call scorecards make it easy to run structured coaching without sitting in on every call.

Chorus integrates deeply with Salesforce, Salesloft, Outreach, and major CRMs, making it a natural fit for companies that already have a sales tech stack.

**Best for:** Enterprise sales organisations that want coaching at scale and conversation intelligence tied to pipeline performance.

**Pricing:** Paid only. Pricing on request (typically $1,000+/user/year at enterprise scale).

---

### 7. Modjo - Best for SMB Sales Teams

Modjo delivers many of the same capabilities as Chorus but at a price point and complexity level that works for smaller sales teams. It records and transcribes calls, identifies key topics, and gives managers a dashboard of what their reps are saying across all conversations.

The smart summary format focuses on what matters for sales: objections raised, competitors mentioned, next steps agreed, and key moments of the call. The topic-based filtering makes it easy to find all calls where pricing was discussed or where a specific competitor came up.

Modjo is notably faster to implement than enterprise platforms - most teams are up and running within a day.

**Best for:** Sales teams of 5 to 50 reps who want conversation intelligence without enterprise complexity or pricing.

**Pricing:** Paid only. Pricing on request, significantly below Chorus at SMB scale.

---

### 8. Notta - Best for Pure Transcription Volume

Notta is built around one core capability: fast, accurate transcription. It handles audio files, live meetings, and even YouTube videos, producing transcripts in 58 languages with one-click translation. The simplicity is the point - there is no meeting bot, no scoring, no complex workflows. You paste a link, upload a file, or open a live meeting, and you get a clean transcript and summary.

For researchers, journalists, legal teams, and anyone who processes large volumes of audio content rather than running team meetings, Notta is one of the most cost-effective tools available.

**Best for:** High-volume transcription workflows, researchers, content creators, and legal professionals.

**Pricing:** Free plan with 120 minutes/month. Paid from $13.99/month.

---

### 9. Tactiq - Best for Zoom and Google Meet Users

Tactiq is a browser extension that works directly inside Zoom, Google Meet, and Microsoft Teams - no separate app to install. It transcribes the meeting live in a sidebar panel, highlights keywords, and lets you add manual notes directly within the transcript as the conversation progresses.

After the call, Tactiq generates a summary and lets you push notes directly to Notion, Jira, Salesforce, or Slack in one click. The keyword highlighting feature is useful for staying focused during long calls - you can mark terms to watch for and see when they appear.

**Best for:** Individuals who want minimal setup and direct Zoom/Meet integration without switching to a new app.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Paid from $12/month.

---

### 10. Airgram - Best for Collaborative Meeting Prep

Airgram stands out by treating meetings as a workflow, not just a recording. Before the meeting, teams use the shared agenda builder to prepare discussion points together. During the call, the AI transcribes while participants add notes and action items inline. After the call, everything is organised into a shareable meeting doc that serves as the source of truth.

This collaborative approach reduces duplicated note-taking and ensures everyone walks out of the meeting with the same record. Airgram integrates with Google Calendar and Outlook for automatic scheduling and works across Zoom and Google Meet.

**Best for:** Teams that want structured meeting management from preparation through follow-up, not just recording.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Paid from $18/user/month.

## Should You Use a Bot-Based or Bot-Free Tool?

This is the most common question when choosing a meeting tool. The short answer: if you run external client calls, a bot-free tool like Jamie, Read AI, or Notta is often the better choice. Many clients find a visible bot participant intrusive, and some organisations have policies against third-party recording bots.

For internal team meetings, the bot approach is generally fine and often preferable - it joins automatically, never misses a meeting, and works hands-free.

## Final Verdict

For most professionals, **Meetgeek** offers the best balance of automation, integrations, and pricing. For sales teams, **Modjo** (SMB) or **Chorus** (enterprise) will deliver the most value with their sales-specific AI analysis. For anyone who needs an invisible assistant that works everywhere, **Jamie** is the cleanest option.

The best approach is to start with a free trial of your top choice, run it on 10 real meetings, and evaluate the summary quality on content you already know well. That real-world test tells you more than any feature comparison.
