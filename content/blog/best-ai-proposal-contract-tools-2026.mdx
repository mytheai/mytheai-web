Every business sends proposals and signs contracts. But the manual version of that process - copy-pasting from old documents, emailing PDFs back and forth, chasing signatures, filing in shared drives - costs teams hundreds of hours per year and introduces errors that can have real consequences.

In 2026, AI-powered proposal and contract tools have made this workflow significantly faster and more reliable. The best tools do not just add e-signature to a Word document. They automate the entire lifecycle: generating first drafts, tracking engagement, managing negotiation, collecting payment, and archiving with extracted terms that can be searched and reported on.

This guide covers the best proposal and contract tools for different team sizes and use cases.

## Proposal Tools: Winning Business Faster

### PandaDoc - Best All-in-One for Most Teams

PandaDoc is the most versatile tool on this list, covering proposals, quotes, contracts, and HR documents in a single platform with a generous free tier. The AI assistant drafts proposal sections from a brief description, and the drag-and-drop editor handles pricing tables, e-signature fields, images, and video embeds without needing a designer.

The key advantage for sales teams is real-time engagement tracking: you see exactly when a prospect opened the document, which sections they read, and whether they forwarded it to a decision-maker. That signal tells you precisely when to follow up rather than guessing.

PandaDoc integrates with Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive, and most CRMs to pull contact and deal data directly into documents - eliminating copy-paste errors on pricing and terms. E-signature is included at no extra cost.

**Best for:** Sales teams, small businesses, HR teams, anyone who needs a complete proposal-to-signature workflow at reasonable cost.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Paid plans from $19/user/month.

---

### Proposify - Best for Agencies with High Volume

Proposify is built for teams that send the same types of proposals repeatedly - marketing agencies, design studios, consulting firms, IT service providers. The content library stores approved copy, case studies, pricing tables, and legal terms that any team member can pull into a new proposal in seconds.

The standout feature for managers is the approval workflow: proposals with non-standard pricing or scope go to a manager for review before being sent, preventing junior team members from making commitments the business cannot keep. Analytics show which proposal templates have the highest close rates and which sections prospects skip - useful data for improving what you send.

**Best for:** Agencies and service businesses sending branded proposals at volume with team-level consistency controls.

**Pricing:** From $49/user/month. No free plan.

---

### Qwilr - Best for Interactive, Web-Based Proposals

Qwilr converts proposals into interactive web pages rather than PDF attachments. Prospects open a URL in their browser, see a polished branded page with embedded video, and can configure pricing options in real time before accepting. When they are ready, they click to accept and pay a deposit through Stripe - without printing, signing, or scanning anything.

This format works particularly well with buyers who are used to modern SaaS products: it signals professionalism and reduces the friction of acceptance. Qwilr also allows you to update a live proposal after sending, which matters when pricing or scope needs to change during negotiation.

**Best for:** Agencies, SaaS companies, and consultants presenting configurable service packages to modern buyers.

**Pricing:** From $35/user/month. No free plan.

---

### Better Proposals - Best for Freelancers and Small Teams

Better Proposals is the most affordable full-featured option for individuals and small agencies. At $19/user/month, it includes proposal templates, e-signature, engagement tracking, and Stripe payment collection. The AI writing assistant helps draft scope and pricing sections from a project brief.

The template library covers web design, marketing, coaching, consulting, and photography among others. For freelancers who need to look professional without paying enterprise prices, it covers everything that matters.

**Best for:** Freelancers, independent consultants, and small agencies who want professional proposals without high per-seat costs.

**Pricing:** From $19/user/month. No free plan; free trial available.

## Contract Management: From Agreement to Archive

### Oneflow - Best for Live Document Negotiation

Oneflow reinvents how contract negotiation works. Instead of sending a PDF and receiving a marked-up version by email, Oneflow contracts are live documents where both parties edit, comment, and propose changes inside a shared workspace. Both sides see each other's edits in real time, eliminating the confusion of tracking document versions across email threads.

The platform handles the complete lifecycle - template creation, negotiation, approval workflow, e-signature, and searchable archive with renewal reminders. A free plan makes it accessible for small teams before committing to paid features.

**Best for:** Sales and procurement teams that negotiate contract terms and want to replace email version chaos with real-time collaboration.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Paid from $38/user/month.

---

### Contractbook - Best for Building a Contract Repository

Contractbook solves a problem most growing companies eventually face: contracts scattered across email, shared drives, and filing cabinets with no systematic way to track what has been agreed, when renewals are due, or what obligations exist.

The platform creates a structured contract repository where every agreement is stored with metadata, linked to counterparties, and searchable by term, value, or date. The AI data extraction feature reads existing contracts and pulls key terms into the database, making it practical to onboard a backlog of historical agreements. Automated renewal alerts prevent contracts from auto-renewing unnoticed.

**Best for:** Operations and legal teams at growing companies replacing ad hoc contract filing with a structured system.

**Pricing:** From $99/month. No free plan.

## Enterprise Contract Tools

For larger organisations with complex approval chains, multiple contract types across jurisdictions, and formal legal operations functions, two tools stand above the rest.

**Juro** treats contracts as structured data rather than documents - every clause and commercial term is stored as a searchable, reportable data point. This allows legal teams to answer questions like "which supplier agreements renew in Q3" or "which contracts include a most-favoured-nation clause" in seconds. Self-service workflows let business teams generate routine contracts like NDAs and employment agreements independently, reducing the bottleneck on the legal team.

**Ironclad** is the enterprise operating system for legal teams, used by Dropbox, Mastercard, and L'Oreal. Its visual workflow designer maps out the complete lifecycle of each contract type with branching logic for different scenarios and jurisdictions. AI playbook review flags deviations from standard positions before a contract is sent, and the contract repository extracts terms automatically for search and reporting.

Both require custom enterprise pricing and a sales conversation before you can start.

## RFP Response: Loopio

A special mention for teams that respond to Requests for Proposal at volume - a time-consuming process that most proposal tools do not address.

**Loopio** builds a searchable library of approved responses to common RFP questions. When a new RFP arrives, AI automatically matches questions to existing library responses and pre-fills the document. The team reviews and refines rather than writing from scratch. Unanswered or outdated questions are routed to the right subject matter expert with deadline tracking. Used by enterprise sales and pre-sales teams where RFP volume justifies a dedicated workflow.

## How to Choose

**Start with PandaDoc if you are unsure.** The free plan covers the core proposal and signature workflow for most small teams. If you outgrow it, the paid plan is affordable and the CRM integrations handle volume.

**Choose Qwilr if your buyers are modern and you sell configurable services.** The web-based format and interactive pricing can meaningfully increase acceptance rates compared to PDF attachments.

**Choose Proposify if you run an agency.** The content library, approval workflows, and analytics are built specifically for high-volume, brand-consistent proposal operations.

**Choose Oneflow or Contractbook once you are managing contracts at scale.** Proposal tools handle the sell side; contract management tools handle the post-signature lifecycle - renewals, obligations, and the searchable archive you will need when questions arise.
