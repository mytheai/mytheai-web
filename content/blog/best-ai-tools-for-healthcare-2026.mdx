Artificial intelligence is transforming healthcare faster than almost any other sector. Physicians today spend more time on documentation than with patients - studies show clinicians spend up to two hours on administrative tasks for every hour of direct patient care. AI is changing that equation.

From ambient documentation tools that silently write notes during consultations to radiology AI that flags critical findings before a radiologist opens the scan, healthcare AI is now embedded across the care pathway. This guide covers the 10 best AI tools for healthcare and medical teams in 2026 - with honest assessments of where each one fits.

---

## AI Clinical Documentation

The largest category of healthcare AI by adoption, clinical documentation tools reduce the administrative burden of note-writing, freeing physicians to focus on patients.

### Nabla

[Nabla](/tools/nabla) is an ambient AI documentation platform that passively listens to physician-patient conversations and generates structured clinical notes automatically. No dictation required - the AI works in the background, capturing the encounter and outputting SOAP notes, referral letters, and after-visit summaries. Nabla integrates with Epic, Athenahealth, and other major EHRs and is used by over 45,000 clinicians.

Best for: Outpatient clinics, primary care, and specialists who want completely hands-free documentation.

### Suki AI

[Suki AI](/tools/suki-ai) takes a voice-first approach: physicians speak commands to navigate their EHR and dictate clinical notes using natural language. Unlike pure ambient tools, Suki gives physicians explicit control over what is captured and how it is structured. It integrates with Epic, Cerner, and eClinicalWorks and has a mobile app for on-call and rounding workflows.

Best for: Physicians who prefer active voice control and explicit note structure over passive ambient capture.

### Nuance DAX

[Nuance DAX](/tools/nuance-dax) (Dragon Ambient eXperience) is Microsoft's enterprise ambient AI documentation solution. Built on decades of Dragon voice recognition technology and now powered by Azure OpenAI, DAX generates complete clinical notes from ambient conversation and integrates natively with Epic, Oracle Health, and other enterprise EHRs. Nuance DAX is the dominant solution in large health systems.

Best for: Enterprise health systems and academic medical centres already in the Microsoft ecosystem.

## AI for Inpatient and Hospital Medicine

### Abridge

[Abridge](/tools/abridge) is a conversation AI platform designed for hospital medicine and specialist care. Built with the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center, it records and transcribes clinical conversations, generating physician notes that are reviewed and signed within the EHR. Abridge is Epic-native with deep integration, and its academic research foundation gives it strong clinical validation.

Best for: Academic medical centres, hospitalists, and specialists seeking research-grade documentation with Epic integration.

### Regard

[Regard](/tools/regard) is an AI diagnostician that works inside Epic, continuously monitoring hospitalized patients and surfacing missed diagnoses and billing opportunities. As a physician reviews a patient, Regard displays suggested diagnoses with supporting evidence, helping hospitalists manage complex patients and ensuring accurate ICD coding for billing accuracy.

Best for: Hospitalist teams and inpatient medicine physicians managing high-volume complex cases on Epic.

## Clinical Decision Support AI

### Glass Health

[Glass Health](/tools/glass-health) is a clinical reasoning AI that assists physicians in building differential diagnoses, assessment plans, and treatment summaries from brief patient descriptions. Physicians enter a clinical vignette, and Glass Health returns structured differentials with explanations - particularly useful for complex presentations, teaching, and second-opinion consultations.

Best for: Physicians working through complex diagnoses, medical education, and case documentation.

### Corti

[Corti](/tools/corti) is a real-time AI that listens during emergency calls and clinical encounters, detecting patterns that suggest critical diagnoses like cardiac arrest, stroke, and sepsis. Emergency dispatchers and clinicians receive real-time alerts as the AI processes the conversation, supporting faster triage decisions. Corti is used in emergency services across Europe and North America.

Best for: Emergency departments, urgent care, and emergency dispatch centres requiring real-time triage support.

## Radiology and Imaging AI

### Aidoc

[Aidoc](/tools/aidoc) is an AI radiology platform that analyzes CT scans, MRIs, and X-rays in real time as they are acquired, prioritizing urgent studies and flagging critical findings. It detects pulmonary embolism, intracranial hemorrhage, aortic dissection, and other time-sensitive conditions before the on-call radiologist opens the worklist. Aidoc integrates with PACS systems and EHRs across 1,000+ hospitals.

Best for: Radiology departments seeking AI triage to reduce time-to-diagnosis for critical imaging findings.

## Physician Productivity and Networking

### Doximity

[Doximity](/tools/doximity) is the professional network for US physicians, used by over 80% of doctors in the country. Beyond networking, Doximity includes AI-powered dictation for clinical notes, a secure messaging platform, and a telemedicine layer. Its AI features are embedded in workflows clinicians already use daily, making adoption organic rather than forced.

Best for: Physicians looking for AI tools embedded in a platform they already use for networking and peer communication.

## AI Prior Authorization

### Anterior

[Anterior](/tools/anterior) automates prior authorization - one of the most time-consuming administrative workflows in healthcare. Its AI reviews clinical documentation against payer policies and submits evidence-based prior auth requests, dramatically reducing the manual effort of obtaining insurance approvals. Anterior integrates with EHRs and payer portals and is used by health systems processing high volumes of prior auth requests.

Best for: Health systems and specialty practices with high prior authorization volumes seeking to reduce administrative overhead.

## Recommended Stacks by Healthcare Setting

**Solo Practice or Small Clinic**
- Documentation: Nabla or Doximity Dictation (lower cost, fast setup)
- Clinical decisions: Glass Health (free tier)
- Patient communication: Doximity Dialer

**Mid-Size Hospital (Epic)**
- Documentation: Abridge or Nuance DAX (both Epic-native)
- Inpatient diagnostics: Regard
- Prior auth: Anterior (if volume justifies)

**Academic Medical Centre**
- Documentation: Abridge (research validation, Epic-native)
- Clinical reasoning: Glass Health + Corti for ED
- Imaging: Aidoc (broad finding coverage)

**Emergency Department**
- Triage support: Corti (real-time, call-based)
- Imaging triage: Aidoc (PE, ICH flagging)
- Documentation: Nuance DAX or Nabla post-encounter

**Health System (Enterprise)**
- Documentation: Nuance DAX (Microsoft ecosystem, all EHRs)
- Radiology: Aidoc
- Prior auth: Anterior
- Networking: Doximity for physician engagement

## What to Look for When Evaluating Healthcare AI

**Clinical validation**: Look for peer-reviewed publications, prospective studies, and FDA clearance where relevant. Aidoc and Corti have strong published evidence bases.

**EHR integration**: Tools that live outside the EHR see lower adoption. Abridge and Regard are Epic-native. Nabla and Suki integrate via API - still solid, but the workflow is slightly different.

**Privacy and HIPAA compliance**: All tools listed here are HIPAA-compliant with BAAs available. Confirm data residency requirements if your organization has specific needs.

**Physician time saved**: The headline metric for documentation AI is time saved per note. Studies show ambient tools like Nabla and Nuance DAX save 1-2 hours of documentation time per physician per day.

**Rollout support**: Enterprise healthcare AI requires change management. Vendors like Nuance and Abridge include implementation support; smaller tools may require more internal effort.

AI is not replacing physicians - it is removing the administrative layer that has grown between clinicians and their patients. The tools above represent the leading solutions deployed in real healthcare settings, with genuine evidence behind the time and quality improvements they deliver.
