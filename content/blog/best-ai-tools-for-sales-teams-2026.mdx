AI has fundamentally changed how sales teams operate. The best sales teams in 2026 are not simply closing more deals than their competitors - they are doing it with fewer reps, lower ramp time, and more predictable forecasting. AI tools now handle the parts of selling that used to consume 40-60% of a rep's week: researching prospects, drafting outreach, logging activity, summarising calls, and flagging deals at risk.

This guide covers the top AI tools across every stage of the sales cycle - from initial prospecting through to closed-won.

---

## Conversation Intelligence

Conversation intelligence tools record, transcribe, and analyse sales calls to surface coaching insights, deal risk signals, and winning patterns at scale.

### Gong

Gong is the market leader in revenue intelligence. It captures every sales conversation - calls, emails, and meetings - and analyses them with AI to identify what separates top-performing reps from average ones, which deals are at risk, and where forecast confidence is highest. Gong's deal board shows every open opportunity with an AI risk score, engagement level, and predicted close date derived from actual conversation activity rather than CRM fields a rep filled in manually.

Sales managers use Gong to coach reps with evidence from real calls - pointing to the specific moment a rep lost momentum or failed to ask a discovery question - rather than relying on rep self-reporting or gut feel. Gong Forecast replaces the weekly pipeline review with an AI-generated forecast that updates in real time.

Used by over 4,000 companies including Snowflake, Hubspot, and LinkedIn.

### Key alternatives
- **Outreach Kaia** - real-time in-call guidance and action item capture, built into Outreach
- **Salesloft Conversation Intelligence** - call recording and coaching powered by the Drift acquisition

## Sales Execution Platforms

Sales execution platforms combine email sequencing, call workflows, LinkedIn steps, and pipeline management in one system - the operating layer the entire sales team runs on.

### Outreach

Outreach is the leading enterprise sales execution platform, used by revenue teams at Zoom, DocuSign, and Snowflake. It combines sales engagement (email sequences, call workflows, LinkedIn steps), deal management (pipeline visibility with AI-predicted close dates), and conversation intelligence (call recording and coaching) in one platform.

Outreach Kaia provides real-time talk track guidance during calls, captures action items automatically, and flags deal risk signals. The forecasting module uses machine learning on historical deal data to produce quarterly revenue predictions that have consistently outperformed human judgment in independent studies.

Best for: enterprise sales organisations with 20+ reps that want a systematic, data-driven sales process across the full revenue cycle.

### Salesloft

Salesloft's Rhythm feature is its differentiated capability: it aggregates signals from across your revenue stack - CRM updates, email engagement, call outcomes, and intent data from third-party providers - and surfaces the highest-priority actions for each rep every morning. Reps start their day knowing exactly which accounts to work and why, rather than scrolling through a static task list.

Salesloft AI generates email drafts, summarises calls, identifies deal risk early (flagging deals with no recent activity, missing economic buyers, or stalled stages), and provides coaching recommendations for managers. Full pipeline generation, deal management, and forecasting in one platform.

## CRM Platforms

The best CRM for your team depends on whether you need a simple pipeline manager, a relationship intelligence layer, or a full all-in-one platform.

### Pipedrive

Pipedrive is a sales CRM built for teams that sell actively and need a clear pipeline view of every deal. Its visual pipeline board shows every open deal, stage probability, and stale deal at a glance. The AI sales assistant suggests next actions based on deal history, and the email writing assistant drafts personalised outreach from within the CRM.

Strong choice for B2B teams of 5-200 people who want a CRM focused on closing rather than CRM administration.

### Close

Close (Close CRM) is built for inside sales teams that sell through phone, email, and video. It includes a built-in power dialer, predictive dialer, SMS, and email sequences in a single platform - so reps can call prospects, auto-drop voicemails, and send follow-up emails without leaving the CRM. All features included at every pricing tier with no feature gating.

Particularly strong for SaaS companies with outbound motions and for recruiting agencies.

### Attio

Attio takes a fundamentally different approach: instead of requiring manual data entry, it builds the CRM automatically from your connected email and calendar. Contacts are enriched with company data, funding rounds, and job titles from public sources. The data model is fully customisable - create the structure that fits your business rather than adapting to a predefined schema.

Popular with venture-backed startups and growth-stage companies that want modern tooling without legacy overhead.

### Affinity

Affinity is relationship intelligence built for dealmakers - VCs, investment bankers, private equity, and enterprise BD teams. It automatically maps who knows whom across your entire team's email and calendar history, scores relationship strength, and surfaces warm introduction paths to target companies. If your business runs on who you know, Affinity keeps that asset visible and current.

## Outbound Prospecting and Sequencing

### Lemlist

Lemlist runs personalised email, LinkedIn, and phone sequences from a single campaign builder. Its AI writes full outreach sequences from a prompt, personalises images and videos in emails at scale based on prospect data, and includes a built-in lead database of 450M+ contacts. The deliverability dashboard monitors sender reputation and inbox placement across connected mailboxes.

Used by sales teams and agencies running multichannel outbound campaigns where personalisation at scale is the competitive advantage.

### Reply.io

Reply.io covers the full outbound workflow: prospect finding, email sequence automation, LinkedIn outreach, phone calls, and AI-generated personalisation. Jason AI (Reply's built-in assistant) generates personalised sequences from a description of the target audience, handles follow-up replies automatically for common objections, and books meetings directly into the calendar. Includes a prospect database with 140M+ contacts and unlimited mailboxes with deliverability tools.

Strong alternative to Outreach and Salesloft for mid-market teams that need automation without enterprise pricing.

---

## Email Coaching

### Lavender

Lavender lives inside Gmail and Outlook and gives real-time feedback on cold emails as you write them. It scores your email on reply-rate predictors - personalisation, reading level, length, subject line, and call to action - and explains exactly what to improve. Prospect data from LinkedIn, recent news, and company announcements is pulled directly into the compose window so you can personalise based on what the prospect published this week.

Lavender claims a 3x improvement in reply rates for active users. Best for SDRs and AEs who want AI coaching without replacing their existing email workflow.

## Recommended Stacks by Role

### SDR Team (outbound-heavy)
**Core**: Lemlist or Reply.io (sequences) + Lavender (email coaching) + Apollo or Clay (lead enrichment)
**CRM**: Pipedrive or HubSpot
**Add-on**: Gong (to coach from call recordings if doing high-volume cold calling)

### AE Team (deal-closing focus)
**Core**: Gong (conversation intelligence) + Outreach or Salesloft (deal management)
**CRM**: HubSpot, Salesforce, or Close depending on company size
**Add-on**: Lavender for email personalisation on strategic accounts

### Revenue Operations
**Foundation**: Gong or Outreach with forecasting enabled
**CRM**: Salesforce (enterprise) or HubSpot (mid-market)
**Enrichment**: Clay or Apollo
**Reporting**: Outreach or Salesloft built-in analytics + your BI tool

### Early-Stage Startup (1-10 reps)
**Minimum stack**: Close (CRM + calling) + Lemlist (outbound) + Lavender (email coaching)
**When to add**: Gong once call volume is high enough to get AI training value

## What to Look for in AI Sales Tools

**Data integration**: The best sales tools connect to your existing CRM, email, and calendar without requiring manual sync. Attio and Affinity do this automatically. Outreach and Salesloft require Salesforce or HubSpot as the source of truth.

**AI quality**: Conversation intelligence is only as good as the data volume behind it. Gong needs high call volume to surface statistically meaningful patterns. Smaller teams get less value from enterprise-tier intelligence tools.

**Workflow fit**: A power dialer in Close has no value for a team doing 90% email outreach. A full sales execution platform like Outreach is overkill for a three-person startup. Match the tool to the actual workflow, not the feature list.

**Pricing model**: Outreach and Salesloft use custom enterprise pricing. Pipedrive and Close have transparent per-seat pricing. Lemlist and Reply.io charge per mailbox. Gong is volume-based. The total cost of ownership varies significantly across team sizes.

The best AI sales stack is the one your reps will actually use. Start with one tool in each category, get adoption before adding complexity, and review quarterly as your team size and motion evolve.
