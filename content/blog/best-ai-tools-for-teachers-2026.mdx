AI has arrived in K-12 classrooms - not as a threat to teachers, but as the assistant most educators have needed for years. The best AI tools for teachers in 2026 don't replace professional judgment; they handle the preparation, differentiation, and administrative work that consumes evenings and weekends.

This guide covers 10 tools built specifically for classroom teachers, across lesson planning, student engagement, formative assessment, reading differentiation, and safe AI deployment.

---

## AI Tutoring and Student Support

### Khanmigo - AI Tutor from Khan Academy

[Khanmigo](/tools/khanmigo) is Khan Academy's AI-powered tutor and teaching assistant, built on the Socratic method. For students, it guides rather than tells - asking questions to lead students toward answers rather than providing them directly. This matters: research consistently shows that retrieving knowledge through questioning strengthens retention more than passive reading.

For teachers, Khanmigo functions as a planning assistant. It generates lesson plans, drafts discussion prompts, creates quiz questions from uploaded content, and builds rubrics. Everything stays grounded in Khan Academy's curriculum library, which means the AI stays age-appropriate and pedagogically sound rather than producing generic content.

Khanmigo is also one of the few AI tutoring tools designed for safe deployment with minors. The SAT preparation partnership with College Board adds utility for secondary teachers working with college-bound students.

**Best for:** K-12 tutoring support, SAT prep, teacher lesson planning
**Pricing:** From $4/month for students

---

### Synthesis - AI Maths Tutor for Kids

[Synthesis](/tools/synthesis-tutor) takes a games-based approach to mathematical reasoning. Originally developed for SpaceX's school programme, the platform presents students with adaptive puzzles, strategy challenges, and collaborative problems rather than traditional drill-and-practice exercises.

The difference in approach is significant. Synthesis builds mathematical intuition and problem-solving confidence alongside arithmetic fluency. The AI adapts in real time to each student's performance, adjusting difficulty and presenting new challenge types to maintain productive challenge without frustration.

Synthesis covers arithmetic through pre-algebra with particular depth in fractions, percentages, and multi-step word problems. Parent and teacher dashboards show session data, skill progression, and engagement metrics. The platform has strong independent efficacy data and significant word-of-mouth adoption among parents and homeschooling families.

**Best for:** Supplementary maths for ages 6-14, building problem-solving confidence
**Pricing:** From $22/month per student

## Lesson Planning and Curriculum Creation

### Curipod - Interactive Lessons in Minutes

[Curipod](/tools/curipod) generates complete interactive slide decks for classroom instruction from a single topic prompt. Enter a subject, grade level, and learning objective, and Curipod builds a full lesson including explanatory slides, discussion prompts, polls, drawing activities, and exit ticket questions.

The key difference from a standard AI slide generator is interactivity. Students join from their devices and respond to polls, word clouds, and open-response activities in real time, with responses displayed on the teacher's screen. Formative assessment is built directly into the lesson flow rather than treated as a separate quiz step.

For teachers who spend 60-90 minutes building an interactive lesson from scratch, Curipod's ability to generate a complete, student-ready lesson in under 10 minutes is a genuine time return. The free tier covers most individual teacher needs.

**Best for:** Secondary and higher education interactive lessons, rapid lesson prep
**Pricing:** Free tier available

---

### Almanack - AI Lesson Planning Assistant

[Almanack](/tools/almanack-ai) focuses on curriculum planning depth. Teachers enter a topic, grade level, curriculum standard, and lesson duration, and Almanack produces a complete lesson plan with learning objectives, activity sequences, differentiation suggestions, and discussion questions.

The platform's worksheet generator creates printable student-facing materials - graphic organisers, reading guides, vocabulary activities, and practice problems - alongside each lesson plan. Almanack aligns to Common Core, Next Generation Science Standards, and other major frameworks, which makes it genuinely useful when curriculum documentation is required.

For teachers covering multiple preps or unfamiliar subjects, the ability to generate a full week of curriculum-aligned lessons significantly reduces planning overhead.

**Best for:** Curriculum-aligned lesson planning, multi-prep teachers, new teachers
**Pricing:** Free tier available

---

### Brisk Teaching - AI Inside Google Tools

[Brisk Teaching](/tools/brisk-teaching) is a Chrome extension that adds AI directly inside Google Docs, Google Slides, Google Forms, and YouTube. Rather than switching to a separate planning platform, teachers activate Brisk from within the tools they already use.

The use cases are highly practical. Highlight a student essay in Google Docs and get instant detailed feedback suggestions. Open a YouTube video and generate a comprehension quiz. Paste an article and create a levelled reading activity. The voice-to-note feature captures verbal feedback quickly during marking sessions.

For schools running Google Workspace for Education, Brisk requires no IT involvement and adds AI capability to existing workflows with zero disruption. It is one of the lowest-friction AI adoption paths available for busy classroom teachers.

**Best for:** Google Workspace schools, feedback on student writing, quiz creation from web content
**Pricing:** Free tier available

## Classroom Engagement and Assessment

### Nearpod - Interactive Lessons Across Devices

[Nearpod](/tools/nearpod) synchronises interactive lessons across student devices. Teachers present slides while students follow along on their own phones or laptops, participating in polls, quizzes, collaborative boards, and drawing activities embedded in the lesson flow.

The VR integration is a standout feature rarely matched by comparable tools: 3D scenes and 360-degree field trips can be embedded in lessons for immersive exploration of geographic locations, historical sites, and scientific environments. Nearpod also has a large library of pre-built teacher-created lessons that reduces the need to create content from scratch.

Nearpod integrates with Google Classroom, Canvas, Schoology, and most major LMS platforms. The free tier supports up to 40 students per session, covering most individual classroom needs.

**Best for:** Synchronised interactive lessons, virtual field trips, formative assessment during lectures
**Pricing:** Free tier available (up to 40 students)

---

### ClassPoint - Interactivity Inside PowerPoint

[ClassPoint](/tools/classpoint) is a PowerPoint add-in that turns existing presentations into interactive classroom experiences. Teachers add question slides - multiple choice, short answer, word cloud, drawing activities - directly into their PowerPoint files, and students participate from their devices without creating accounts.

The AI features include automatic quiz generation from slide content and gamification tools that award points and display leaderboards during class. For teachers who have invested years in building PowerPoint slide libraries, ClassPoint adds engagement without requiring them to rebuild or migrate content to a new platform.

ClassPoint is particularly popular in higher education and corporate training environments where PowerPoint is the established standard and instructors want engagement without switching tools.

**Best for:** PowerPoint-based teaching, higher education, adding interactivity to existing presentations
**Pricing:** Free tier available

---

### Conker - Quiz Generation in Seconds

[Conker](/tools/conker) is purpose-built for one workflow: generating formative assessment quickly from any source material. Paste a topic, upload a document, or enter a learning objective, and Conker generates multiple choice, true/false, short answer, and open response questions in seconds.

Quizzes can be shared with students via link without requiring student accounts. Conker supports Google Classroom for assignment distribution and lets teachers target specific Bloom's Taxonomy levels when generating questions. The entire experience is optimised for speed - a 5-minute exit ticket takes under 2 minutes to create and deploy.

For teachers who frequently run quick comprehension checks or review activities, Conker's simplicity is the value. It does one thing very well rather than trying to be a full assessment platform.

**Best for:** Quick exit tickets, comprehension checks, review quizzes for any topic
**Pricing:** Free tier available

## Safe AI Deployment in K-12 Classrooms

### SchoolAI - Teacher-Controlled AI for Students

[SchoolAI](/tools/schoolai) is built specifically for schools that need AI with teacher oversight and compliance safeguards. Teachers create AI-powered learning spaces - configuring the AI's behaviour, topics, and guardrails before students access them. Students interact through guided activities while teachers monitor from a central dashboard.

The compliance architecture is the core differentiator. Student conversations are filtered and visible to teachers at all times. The platform is designed to comply with COPPA and FERPA requirements, which resolves the most common barrier to school adoption. SchoolAI integrates with Google Classroom and Microsoft Teams for easy assignment distribution.

For districts searching for AI tools that can be deployed within compliance constraints, SchoolAI provides a structured environment that keeps the AI within the teacher's pedagogical framework rather than giving students uncontrolled access to general-purpose AI.

**Best for:** School district AI deployment, compliant student AI access, teacher-monitored AI interaction
**Pricing:** Free tier for individual teachers; paid plans for districts

## Reading Differentiation

### Diffit - Levelled Reading for Every Student

[Diffit](/tools/diffit) addresses one of the most time-consuming parts of teaching mixed-ability classes: differentiating the same content for students reading at different levels. Teachers paste in an article, URL, or topic, and Diffit generates multiple versions of the same content - simplified for struggling readers, grade-level for most students, enriched for advanced learners - each with comprehension questions tailored to the reading level.

The platform also supports English language learners by generating content in multiple languages and at reduced complexity levels. Vocabulary lists, graphic organisers, and fill-in-the-blank activities accompany each text version. For teachers in mixed-ability classrooms, producing differentiated materials no longer means doubling preparation time.

**Best for:** Mixed-ability classrooms, English language learners, reading comprehension activities
**Pricing:** Free tier available

## What to Consider Before Adopting

**Compliance first.** For K-12 districts, COPPA and FERPA compliance is non-negotiable. SchoolAI is purpose-built for this. General AI tools like ChatGPT or Claude require policy and parental consent frameworks before student use.

**Start with one workflow.** Teachers who adopt AI most successfully start with a single pain point - lesson planning, quiz creation, or student feedback - rather than trying to replace the entire workflow at once. Pick the tool that solves your most time-consuming task first.

**Free tiers cover most individual needs.** Eight of the ten tools above offer meaningful free plans. Most teachers can test and integrate AI tools without budget approval.

The goal of every tool on this list is the same: give teachers back time they can spend on the parts of teaching that only humans can do.
