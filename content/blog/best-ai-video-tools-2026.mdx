Video has become the default content format. Whether you are a solo creator, a marketing team, or a media company, producing video at scale used to require a production crew, editing expertise, and significant budget. In 2026, AI has changed each stage of that workflow.

This guide covers the best AI video tools across generation, editing, voiceover, and repurposing  -  with clear recommendations for who each tool fits.

## AI Video Generation

For text-to-video generation, three tools dominate the professional tier.

**[Runway](/tools/runway)** is the most feature-complete platform. Runway Gen-3 Alpha produces high-quality clips with precise camera movement controls, motion brush tools for directing specific areas of the frame, and a full suite of post-generation editing tools. It is the preferred choice for advertising agencies, filmmakers, and studios where output quality is non-negotiable. The trade-off is complexity  -  Runway takes time to learn and credits deplete quickly on longer or higher-resolution generations.

**[Kling AI](/tools/kling)** is the standout for physical realism. Developed by Kuaishou, Kling generates clips up to two minutes long  -  significantly longer than most competitors  -  with physics simulation that produces natural movement for water, fabric, and human motion. It is particularly strong for product demonstrations and narrative content where realistic physics matters more than stylistic control.

**[Luma AI](/tools/luma-ai)** (Dream Machine) rounds out the top tier with cinematic quality and smooth motion. Luma's strength is in scenes with complex lighting and camera movement, and it is one of the few generators where results consistently look like they could have been captured rather than generated. The credit-based model makes it accessible to evaluate before committing to a subscription.

**[Pika](/tools/pika)** is the best option for creators who prioritise speed over maximum quality. Clips generate faster than Runway, the interface is simpler, and the results are consistently good for social-first short-form content. If you are producing Instagram Reels and TikTok content rather than advertising campaigns, Pika is the more efficient choice.

## Video Editing with AI

**[Descript](/tools/descript)** changed what video editing means for non-editors. The entire editing workflow is text-based: the video is transcribed automatically, and editing the transcript edits the video. Deleting a word removes it from the footage. Deleting a sentence removes it completely. Filler words ("um", "uh", "like") can be removed in one click across an entire recording. For podcasters, interviewers, and anyone producing talking-head content, Descript eliminates the most time-consuming parts of editing entirely.

**[Captions](/tools/captions-ai)** is the equivalent tool for short-form creators. Where Descript is a full editor, Captions is focused on the specific workflow of recording to camera and publishing to social. Its Eye Contact Correction feature adjusts the speaker's gaze to maintain direct eye contact even when reading from notes  -  a subtle but impactful improvement for creators who use scripts or teleprompters. One-click filler word removal and animated subtitle styles designed for retention make it a powerful tool for anyone posting regularly on TikTok, Reels, or Shorts.

## Video Repurposing

**[Opus Clip](/tools/opus-clip)** is the tool that turns long-form content into short-form gold. Upload a webinar, podcast recording, or YouTube video, and Opus Clip identifies the most engaging moments, clips them, resizes them for vertical formats, burns in animated captions, and ranks them by predicted virality. What takes hours of manual editing takes minutes. For content teams that produce long-form material but need to maintain a presence on short-form platforms, Opus Clip is one of the highest-ROI tools available.

**[InVideo AI](/tools/invideo-ai)** handles the reverse workflow  -  turning text into video. Input a script or topic, and InVideo assembles a complete video with stock footage, AI voiceover, captions, and music. It is not a replacement for filmed content, but for evergreen explainer videos, social media content, and high-volume marketing assets, it eliminates the filming step entirely.

## AI Voiceover for Video

**[ElevenLabs](/tools/elevenlabs)** produces the most natural-sounding AI voices available and is the standard for creators who want a consistent branded voice across content. The voice cloning feature creates a custom voice from a short audio sample, enabling creators to generate narration in their own voice without recording every line.

**[Murf](/tools/murf)** is the better choice when the priority is production workflow rather than voice realism. The built-in video timeline editor syncs voiceover to footage, adds background music, and manages timing  -  all within the same interface. For corporate video production and e-learning, Murf's studio tools reduce the number of applications in the workflow.

## Building Your AI Video Stack

**For social media creators:** Captions (capture + edit) + Opus Clip (repurpose) + Suno (background music). Budget: $25–40 per month.

**For marketing teams:** InVideo AI (volume production) + Runway (hero campaign content) + ElevenLabs (consistent voice). Budget: $70–120 per month.

**For podcasters and long-form creators:** Riverside (recording) + Descript (editing) + Opus Clip (repurposing clips). Budget: $50–60 per month.

Start with the tool that addresses your biggest bottleneck. If filming is the constraint, InVideo AI or a generator removes it. If editing is the constraint, Descript or Captions removes it. If distribution is the constraint, Opus Clip removes it.

> **Verdict:** Runway and Kling AI lead on generation quality. Descript leads on editing. Opus Clip leads on repurposing. For most teams, combining one tool from each category delivers a complete AI video workflow at under $100 per month.
